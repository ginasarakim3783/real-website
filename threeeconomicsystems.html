Three Economic Systems

Economic Systems are a way used by society to produce and distribute goods and services. They exist because survival depends on the ability to provide basic necessities such as food, clothing, and shelter, which economic systems decide. The type of economic system is determined by how a country answers three questions: what goods and services will be produced, how will the goods and services be produced, and who will consume the goods and services produced. Currrently, there are three types of economic systems which are a traditional economy, command economy, and market economy. There is no perfect type of economy because each one has its strengths and weaknesses, it is up to the people to decide what factors are expendable and what factors are nonnegotiable to the economy.

Type of Economy Strengths Weaknesses
Traditional Economy
  1. everyone knows their role
  2. little uncertainty over what to produce and how to produce it
  3. whom to produce for is answered by custom
  4. life is generally stable and unpredictable
  1. tends to discourage new ideas
  2. there is lack of progress which leads to a lower standard of living
Command Economy
  1. can change the economy drastically in a short period of time.
  1. economy is not designed to meet needs and wants of the population.
  2. there is no incentive to work hard which can lead to unexpected results.
  3. a large bureaucracy in a command economy can lead to corruption.
  4. economy is not flexible in dealing with day to day problems.
  5. new and unique ideas are stifled.
Market Economy
  1. market can adjust over time
  2. freedom for everyone involved
  3. decentralized decision making
  4. variety of goods leads to greater satisfaction
  1. trouble deciding who to produce for
  2. young, old, and sick people will have difficulty surviving
  3. market sometimes fails
