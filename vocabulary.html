Vocabulary

Unit 1

  1. Needs: what is necessary to survive
  2. Wants: way to express needs and goods consumed beyond what is necessary for survival.
  3. Goods: physical objects that can be purchased
  4. Services: actions performed for a fee
  5. Economics: study of how resources are allocated to satisfy limitless wants.
  6. Opportunity Cost: what is sacrificed when one choice is made over the "next best alternative"
  7. Trade Off: exchange for one thing in return for another.
  8. Productive Efficiency: when products are produced in the cheapest way possible.
  9. Allocative Efficiency: when products produced are the most desirable in a society.
  10. Constant Opportunity Cost: resources taht are easily adaptable for producing either good.
  11. Absolute Advantage: where an individual can produce more of a specific commodity than another individual using the same amount of resources.
  12. Comparative Advantage: where an individual can produce a specific commodity at a lower opportunity cost at a lower opportunity cost than another individual.
  13. Heckter-Olin Theory: we benefit from trade through comparative advantage and when there is a different mix between labor and capital.
  14. Specialization: producing according to comparative advantage.
  15. Economic System: method used by a society to produce and distribute goods and services.
  16. Free Market Economy: people act in their own best interest to answer economic questions.
  17. Capitalism: system where people own their own labor, property, equipment, and resources to make money.
  18. Contract: agreement between people to buy and sell each other's goods, parties are legally bound to keep agreements (either said or written).
